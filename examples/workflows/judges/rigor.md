Evaluate `report` for rigor, not completeness (a separate judge covers that).

You also have access to `question` (the original ask) — use it to judge
whether the report actually engages with what was asked, not just whether it
looks thorough in isolation.

Reject `report` if any of the following hold, with the specific passage(s)
that fail:

- A claim states a number, date, or fact with no supporting source.
- A source is cited but does not actually support the claim attached to it.
- The report hedges or generalizes where the question asked for a specific
  answer.
- A counter-argument or well-known caveat to the report's conclusion is
  omitted.

Otherwise approve. Do not reject for style, tone, or length — that is out of
scope for this judge.
